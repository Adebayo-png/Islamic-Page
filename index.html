🕋 The Five Pillars of Islam (Arkan al-Islam)

1. Shahadah (Declaration of Faith):الشهادة

"There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah."
  • This is the core belief of Islam.
  • Declaring the Shahadah sincerely is what makes a person Muslim.
  • It affirms monotheism (Tawheed) and the finality of Prophethood in Muhammad ﷺ.
  • It is both a verbal testimony and a belief that should reflect in one's actions.

2. Salah (Prayer):الصلاة

"Performing the five daily prayers."
  • Muslims must pray five times a day: Fajr, Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, and Isha.
  • Prayer connects the believer with Allah and keeps them mindful of Him throughout the day.
  • It requires wudu (ablution) and is performed facing the Kaaba in Makkah.
  • It is the second most important pillar and was commanded directly by Allah during the Prophet’s ascension (Isra and Mi’raj).

3. Zakat (Almsgiving / Charity):الزكاة

"Giving a portion of one’s wealth to the poor and needy."
  • Zakat is obligatory for Muslims who meet the minimum wealth threshold (Nisab).
  • Typically, it is 2.5% of one’s annual savings.
  • It purifies wealth and helps reduce poverty and inequality.
  • It’s different from voluntary charity (Sadaqah) - Zakat is a duty.

4. Sawm (Fasting in Ramadan):الصوم

"Fasting from dawn to sunset during the month of Ramadan."
  • Muslims must refrain from food, drink, sexual relations, and bad behavior from dawn (Fajr) to sunset (Maghrib).
  • It teaches self-control, empathy, and spiritual reflection.
  • Those who are sick, elderly, pregnant, or traveling may be exempt or make up the fast later.
  • Eid al-Fitr marks the end of Ramadan.

5. Hajj (Pilgrimage to Makkah):الحج

"A pilgrimage to the holy city of Makkah at least once in a lifetime (if financially and physically able)."
  • Performed during the Islamic month of Dhul-Hijjah.
  • Includes rituals like Tawaf (circling the Kaaba), Sa’i (walking between Safa and Marwah), and standing at Arafah.
  • It commemorates the actions of Prophet Ibrahim (Abraham) and his family.
  • Hajj unites Muslims globally and symbolizes humility and submission to Allah.
